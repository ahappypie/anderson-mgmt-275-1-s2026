# P11: Kelechi Okafor

**Senior Internal Auditor** — Tier-1 Global Bank (London)

**Segment:** Second Line of Defense — Audit

---

## Profile

ACCA-qualified, 10 years in internal audit. Reports to Chief Audit Executive. Performs annual review of all production AI systems.

## Context

Internal audit teams have started building dedicated AI-audit practices. Kelechi is one of three auditors at her bank assigned to AI workflows full-time.

## Current Pain Points

- Asks the AI team for evidence of agent decisions and gets log files in 7 different formats.
- Cannot get clean evidence that 'the policy as written' actually matches 'the policy as enforced.'
- Boards are asking for AI-specific audit opinions she does not yet have the tooling to give.

## Reactions to GAP

### What they would love

> "The 'policy fired, here is which rule, here is the human who signed off' chain — that's exactly the evidence trail I have been begging for."

> "Cryptographic signatures on the log mean I can trust a 12-month-old record without having to verify with the engineering team that no one tampered with it."

> "Denied actions captured alongside approved ones. That changes how I sample. Today I can only audit what was done. Now I can audit what was stopped, which is where I find the interesting findings."

### What they would worry about

> "How do I verify that the policy file under audit at month-end is the same policy that was in force during the events I'm sampling?"

> "Can I get read-only access to the policy version history without going through the AI team?"

> "Coverage. If GAP only governs the actions flagged as high-risk, what assures me the risk classification itself is correct? That's where most audit findings live."

### Would they buy / pilot?

Not a buyer, but a critical reviewer. Her sign-off is required for the bank to call this a 'controlled' system.

## Defining Quote

> **"I trust controls I can sample. Show me how I sample GAP without becoming dependent on the team I'm auditing, and we make progress."**
