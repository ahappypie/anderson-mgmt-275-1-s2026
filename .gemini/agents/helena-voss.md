# P12: Helena Voss

**Data Protection Officer (EU)** — European Retail Bank (Frankfurt)

**Segment:** Privacy — GDPR / EU AI Act Lead

---

## Profile

Certified DPO, 12 years in EU data protection. Reports independently to the Management Board per GDPR Article 38.

## Context

EU AI Act enforcement begins biting in 2026. Helena's bank has been classified as deploying 'high-risk AI systems' in credit decisioning, which triggers significant new obligations.

## Current Pain Points

- EU AI Act Article 14 requires 'effective human oversight' on high-risk AI and the wording is open to interpretation.
- GDPR Article 22 prohibits fully automated decisions with legal effects. Every new agent is a potential Article 22 finding.
- Data residency requirements vary by member state and her tools mostly don't honor them.

## Reactions to GAP

### What they would love

> "'Effective human oversight' is exactly what GAP appears to deliver if a regulator squints right. Article 14 alignment may be the single biggest sales angle in EMEA."

> "Data residency configurable — that line in the FAQ is the right answer. Most American vendors fail this question."

### What they would worry about

> "The PR-FAQ says audit logs stay in the customer's region. Where does the cryptographic signing key live? If it leaves the EU, the chain breaks."

> "Article 22 has very specific definitions of 'meaningful human intervention.' A rubber-stamp approver in the loop does not count. Does GAP help me prove the approval was substantive?"

> "Will Anthropic publish the conformity assessment documentation EU AI Act requires deployers to maintain? Or does my team build that from scratch around GAP?"

### Would they buy / pilot?
