# P14: Naomi Goldberg

**Senior Sanctions Analyst (Tier 2 Reviewer)** — Large US Bank

**Segment:** Frontline Approver — UX End User

---

## Profile

8 years in sanctions screening, certified ACAMS. Sits in compliance under a Compliance Officer. Approves or denies escalated transactions.

## Context

Naomi would be the named human in 'route to a named human approver.' GAP's design directly affects her daily workflow.

## Current Pain Points

- Currently she gets escalations as half-formed emails with missing context. She often has to dig into 3 systems to make a decision.
- Her approval queue spikes unpredictably and her SLA is 30 minutes.
- She is graded on accuracy, not speed, but business partners complain when she's slow.

## Reactions to GAP

### What they would love

> "Reasoning chain attached — yes. If I can see why the agent flagged something, I can decide in 5 minutes instead of 30."

> "Approval in Slack or email instead of a new tool. I will not log into a fifth system."

> "Deny is in v1. That's the half of my job that actually matters. Approving the obvious ones is the easy half. Denying with a clean reason that survives audit review three months later is what I get paid for."

### What they would worry about

> "If the reasoning chain is wrong or misleading, I will rubber-stamp the approval. I'm trained to trust the work in front of me. That is a feature, not a bug, of how compliance teams operate."

> "Deny reasons need to be structured, not a free-text box. Free text means I type 'suspicious' and my QA team has nothing to sample on. Give me a dropdown tied to my bank's deny taxonomy, or let me write one once and reuse it."

> "Will my actions be audited and second-guessed in retrospect more than they are today? More auditability cuts both ways."

### Would they buy / pilot?

Not a buyer. But her enthusiasm matters for compliance officer adoption.

## Defining Quote

> **"Give me the agent's reasoning and a one-click approve in Slack and I'll do 4x the throughput. Give me another web app and I'll route everything to my manager."**
