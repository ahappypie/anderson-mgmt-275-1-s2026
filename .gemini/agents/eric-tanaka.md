# P07: Eric Tanaka

**Director of Claims Operations** — Property & Casualty Insurer

**Segment:** Operations — End Customer of the Policy

---

## Profile

Runs 800 claims adjusters across 4 regional offices. Career claims professional, no technical background. Reports to COO.

## Context

Eric's team uses Claude to draft initial claim assessments. Today the agent drafts and humans execute. He has been told the agent could do it all if compliance signs off.

## Current Pain Points

- His adjusters spend 35% of their time on documentation rather than customer-facing work.
- Cycle time on a routine claim is 11 days; competitors are at 6.
- His SVP wants 25% productivity gain next year without headcount adds.

## Reactions to GAP

### What they would love

> "I don't care about the audit log. I care that compliance stops being the reason I can't ship."

> "If GAP is what makes my agents actually do the work, that's the only feature I need."

### What they would worry about

> "If every action my agent takes has to be approved by a human, I haven't gained anything. The auto-approve path has to be the default for routine claims."

> "My adjusters are going to be the 'approvers' for escalations. Will GAP add work to their queue?"

> "I don't want to be involved in writing policies. That's compliance's job. Tell me what to expect and I'll deliver outcomes."

### Would they buy / pilot?

Not the buyer — but a strong advocate to the compliance team because GAP unblocks his roadmap.

## Defining Quote

> **"I have heard 'compliance is working on it' for two years. If GAP is the thing that closes that loop, I'm in. If it's another layer my team has to learn, I'm out."**
